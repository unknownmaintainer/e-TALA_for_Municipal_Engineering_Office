Document Retrieval Request Policy

The document retrieval services are strictly governed by the local executive code for the Municipal Engineering Office of Carigara. Please follow the instructions below:

Authorized Personnel Access

Only municipal employees, accredited engineers, and authorized officers with active accounts are permitted to download or retrieve official engineering documents. All retrieval activities are logged in the System Audit Trail for compliance purposes.

Process for Certified Copies
  1. Locate the required document utilizing the Advanced Search tool.
  2. Submit a request token generated from the document detail page to the records desk.
  3. For external requests (e.g. property owners, legal queries), a formal written request approved by the Municipal Engineer is required.
Note: Standard processing time for physical retrievals from deep archives is 24-48 business hours. Digital downloads are immediate upon approval.
Official Archive Rules

In compliance with the guidelines of the National Archives of the Philippines (NAP), the Municipal Engineering Office observes the following archive rules for documents:

Record Series Title Active Period Storage Period (Archive) Total Retention Disposition Action
Approved Building Permits 5 Years Permanent Permanent Archives (Permanent Preservation)
Certificate of Occupancy 5 Years Permanent Permanent Archives (Permanent Preservation)
Structural Plans & Blueprints 10 Years Permanent Permanent Archives (Permanent Preservation)
Electrical & Plumbing Permits 5 Years 10 Years 15 Years Destroy after review (if no claims)
Inspector Activity Logs 2 Years 3 Years 5 Years Disposition (Shredding)
Warning: No record may be disposed of, destroyed, or deleted without the explicit written clearance of the National Archives of the Philippines and the Municipal Records Committee.
e-TALA System Archival Policies

The e-TALA operations and system configurations comply with standard ISO 15489 for Information and Documentation — Records Management.

1. Data Integrity and Verification

All uploaded documents must consist of high-resolution digital copies (minimum 300 DPI) and undergo standard validation. The system computes MD5 hashes to prevent duplicate entries and assure data integrity.

2. Role-Based Access Control (RBAC)
  • Admins: Have full access including user registration, setting modifications, and deleting duplicate records.
  • Staff: Can upload, search, view, and update archival descriptions.
  • Engineers: Can view records and print reports.
3. Backup & Disaster Recovery

Database records and media files are automatically backed up daily at 03:00 UTC. Redundant copies are stored off-site utilizing secure government cloud repositories to prevent data loss in the event of local infrastructure failures.

Unauthorized access, attempt to bypass access controls, or utilization of system resources for personal, non-governmental activities is strictly prohibited and subject to legal prosecution under Philippine law (e.g. Republic Act No. 10175 - Cybercrime Prevention Act of 2012).

User Credentials Integrity

Users must maintain the confidentiality of their credentials. Sharing accounts is strictly forbidden. Any activity originating from your account will be considered your direct action. If you suspect your password has been compromised, reset it immediately via e-TALA settings.

Strict Information Confidentiality

Many records housed in e-TALA contain proprietary engineering designs, personal contact information of property owners, and structural plans sensitive to public safety. All details retrieved must be handled with appropriate confidentiality safeguards.

Privacy Policy

Last Updated: June 22, 2026

The Municipal Engineering Office of Carigara is committed to protecting user privacy and ensuring compliance with the Republic Act No. 10173, otherwise known as the Data Privacy Act of 2012 (DPA).

Information Collected
  • Account Details: Full name, official email address, role, and username.
  • System Log Activities: Login timestamps, IP address, accessed records, modified records, and browser details.
  • Owner Details: Metadata associated with archived records, such as applicant names, project addresses, and tax identifiers.
Purpose of Data Processing

Collected details are processed solely to verify access rights, generate audit logs, assure historical record traceability, and support municipal planning and verification services. No data is shared with external commercial third parties.

Data Security Measures

The system implements industry-standard safety measures including AES-256 file encryption, TLS transmission encryption, hashing, and periodic vulnerabilities scanning to prevent unauthorized data leaks, loss, or access.
